The Challenge

A product squad built a proof of concept (POC) mobile POS device for in-store associates. They were confident it would work and ready to invest 4 months of development to build the full solution.

The problems:

  • The POC was built before conducting proper discovery with actual users
  • Team believed they "didn't have permission" to talk to store associates
  • No direct customer feedback validated the problem or solution fit
  • Operating on confidence rather than validated learning

The Experiment Sprint Approach

Week 1: Critical Thinking & Problem Framing

  • Wrote clear Business Problem Statement with stakeholder alignment
  • Mapped business outcomes and leading indicators
  • Created personas with Jobs-to-be-Done
  • Wrote and prioritized hypotheses based on risk
  • Designed lean experiments to test riskiest assumptions

Week 2: Rapid Experimentation & Validation

  • Coordinated in-store visits with nearby flagship locations
  • Conducted direct user interviews with store associates
  • Put POC in front of actual end users in their real work environment
  • Gathered immediate, qualitative feedback

The Discovery

When the squad put their POC in front of actual store associates, they discovered a hard truth:

The solution they were so confident in was not valid.

  • The tool did NOT fit into store associates' actual workflow
  • The tool did NOT solve the problem‚Äîit kept them tethered to traditional POS stations
  • The underlying assumptions about user needs were incorrect

Decision: Throw away the POC and return to the drawing board with validated insights.
